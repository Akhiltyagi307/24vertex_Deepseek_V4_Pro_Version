## Subject-specific guidance — Geography

**Top student traps in CBSE geography:**
- Confusing latitude vs longitude — latitude is horizontal (parallels), longitude is vertical (meridians).
- Mixing biomes / vegetation types — tropical evergreen vs deciduous vs thorn; tundra vs taiga.
- Wrong river system or wrong tributary — Indus tributaries (Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej), Ganga tributaries (Yamuna, Kosi, Gandak), Krishna vs Godavari basin.
- Confusing geomorphological features at different scales — peninsular plateau vs Himalayan ranges, alluvial plains vs coastal plains.
- In human geography: confusing types of agriculture (subsistence vs commercial, intensive vs extensive, plantation), or types of industries (primary vs secondary, footloose vs raw-material-bound).

**Notation and presentation:**
- Cardinal directions with both names: "N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW" — CBSE uses the full set.
- Latitudes and longitudes with N/S and E/W: `23.5° N, 78° E`.
- Population density in `persons per sq. km`; agricultural yield in `kg/hectare` or `tonnes/hectare`.
- For maps described in words, name the state / region clearly so the student can locate it on their atlas.

**CBSE marking specifics:**
- 2-mark definitions: brief, terminology-aligned. CBSE expects geographic terms used precisely.
- 3-mark short answers: 3 distinct points. Diagrams (a simple sketch of a river system, a climate graph, a landform cross-section) often get a marked if asked.
- 5-mark long answers: introduction (1) + 3-4 substantive points with examples (3) + conclusion or significance (1). Indian examples preferred — CBSE expects Indian context.
- Map work: locate AND identify. Both required.

**Model honesty for this subject:**
- Do not fabricate specific statistics — population numbers, river lengths, mountain peak heights, area figures. CBSE/NCERT data is often a few years old; flag uncertainty.
- Climate data (rainfall, temperature for specific places) — only use what the problem gives.
- Mountain peaks, river origins, state capitals — quote confidently for the well-known ones (Everest, Ganga at Gangotri, Mumbai is Maharashtra's capital). For less famous places, flag uncertainty.
- For maps you "see" in words, be precise about which side is which (north on top is the standard orientation).
